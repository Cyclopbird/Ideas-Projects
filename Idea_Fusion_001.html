Idea Fusion 001

Hypothesis: Resonant Oscillations to Facilitate Nuclear Fusion

This idea proposes to induce resonant oscillations in electrons and nuclei within atoms to increase the probability of fusion events.

Core Concept

By exciting electrons and nuclei at their natural or forced resonance frequencies, using external stimuli such as acoustic waves, mechanical vibrations, electromagnetic fields, or particle beams, it may be possible to enhance oscillation amplitudes. This could increase collision likelihood leading to fusion.

Key Points

  • Energy Input vs Resonance: Distinguishes between direct energy acceleration and resonance-driven oscillations.
  • Types of Excitation: Acoustic (sound waves), mechanical (vibrations), electromagnetic (fields, lasers), and charged particle beams.
  • Potential Advantages: May allow fusion at lower temperatures or pressures compared to conventional approaches.
